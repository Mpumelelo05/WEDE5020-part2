We host regular networking events that create valuable opportunities for students to engage with industry professionals, peers, and potential employers in a meaningful way. These events expose students to real-world environments, helping them understand workplace expectations, industry trends, and professional standards.

Through these interactions, students build strong professional relationships, improve communication skills, and gain insight that cannot be learned in a classroom alone. We believe networking plays a crucial role in personal and career development, opening doors to mentorship, internships, and job opportunities.

Beyond the workplace, these connections help students grow in confidence, broaden their perspective, and develop social skills that positively influence their everyday lives and future aspirations.

UBYC Library Brews Up Student Comfort

Library Services at the Ubuntu Youth Club (UBYC) officially launched a new coffee machine service at the Westville campus on 18 March 2026. Although the launch event took place at Westville, machines were installed across all campuses in early March.

The initiative aims to improve access to basic amenities in library spaces and support a more effective study environment. This forms part of UBYC's strategy to transform libraries into modern social learning spaces.

These spaces support academic work while enhancing student comfort and well-being. Students can study, research, and collaborate more effectively without leaving the library frequently.

The service is especially beneficial for postgraduate students who spend long hours in the library. The cashless system allows quick payments via card or tap.

Drinks include white chocolate, latte, and caramel-flavoured beverages. Pricing ranges between R11 and R15, making it affordable for students.

Future plans include expanding to cold drinks and snack vending machines to further enhance convenience in library spaces.
